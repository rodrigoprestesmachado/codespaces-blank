Metacognition Model of Flavel

An overview of the key components of metacognition.

Metacognitive Knowledge

  • Person
  • Task
  • Strategy

Metacognitive Knowledge: Person

Understanding of beliefs, strengths, and weaknesses.

  • What strengths can you rely on, and which areas require extra attention?
  • How have your beliefs shaped your approach to this task?

Metacognitive Knowledge: Task

Understanding the degree of difficulty of a task.

  • What about this task makes it easy or challenging for you?
  • How do you decide how much time and effort is needed?

Metacognitive Knowledge: Strategy

Understanding strategies that can help retain knowledge.

  • Which strategy has worked for you in similar tasks, and why?
  • How could a different approach help you overcome obstacles?

Metacognitive Experiences

  • Reflections
  • Feelings of confidence or uncertainty

Metacognitive Experiences: Reflections

Reflecting on progress and adjusting strategies.

  • How has your understanding of the material evolved?
  • In what ways do you need to adjust strategies for efficiency?

Metacognitive Experiences: Confidence

Feelings of confidence or uncertainty during task completion.

  • How confident do you feel about your approach?
  • When do you realize that you might need to change strategy?

Goals (or tasks)

Goal setting helps regulate cognition and guide strategies.

  • What specific objectives can guide your task completion?
  • How can breaking the task into smaller goals improve focus?

Actions (or Strategies)

  • Planning
  • Monitoring
  • Evaluation

Planning

Establishing a plan for task completion.

  • What steps can you create to stay on track?
  • How can you address potential challenges?

Monitoring

Continuously checking for understanding during the task.

  • How do you know you are on the right track?
  • What signals will indicate you need to adjust?

Evaluation

Reflecting on task performance post-completion.

  • How can you assess the effectiveness of your strategies?
  • What lessons can you apply to future tasks?
